Flash & Critique

Brief Encounters with Media
Screen

The Zone of Interest

Dir. Jonathan Glazer
A masterclass in the banality of evil. Glazer refuses to show us the horror, forcing us instead to listen to it from the garden next door. It is a film about compartmentalization, and how easily we tune out the screaming.
Verdict: Essential Viewing

Poor Things

Dir. Yorgos Lanthimos
A baroque, steampunk fever dream of feminist awakening. Emma Stone throws herself into the physical comedy of an infant brain in an adult body with reckless abandon. Beautiful, grotesque, and unexpectedly sweet.
Verdict: Joyfully Weird

Scavengers Reign

Showrunner: Joseph Bennett
Biological science fiction at its peak. The ecosystem is the antagonist here. It doesn't hate the survivors; it just wants to digest them. A stunning animated meditation on adaptation and symbiosis.
Verdict: Hypnotic
Page

Septology

Jon Fosse
A prayer in slow motion. Fosse’s "slow prose" creates a hypnotic rhythm that dissolves the boundary between the narrator and the reader. It is about God, art, and the alternate lives we didn't lead. Requires patience, rewards with transcendence.
Verdict: Meditative

Lapvona

Ottessa Moshfegh
Moshfegh abandons her bored urbanites for a medieval village defined by depravity, cannibalism, and magical thinking. It is disgusting, cruel, and oddly funny, but ultimately feels like a painting of hell with no exit.
Verdict: Divisive

North Woods

Daniel Mason
A house in New England is the main character, witnessing centuries of lovers, apples, ghosts, and beetles. A kaleidoscope of genres—poems, letters, case files—that proves the land remembers us longer than we remember it.
Verdict: Haunting
